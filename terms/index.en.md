## Deleting your content

You can delete your own submissions within a time limit: an on-time submission locks in once its challenge's window ends (galleries, streaks, and the leaderboard are built on them), while late and community challenge submissions can be self-deleted for about a week. A community challenge can be deleted by its author until it has submissions or becomes an official weekly challenge.

If you want something removed that you can no longer delete yourself, just [contact me](#contact). See also the data removal note in the [privacy policy](/privacy).

## Rules

- Submissions should be your own handwriting of the challenge text, whether physical or on a tablet. No typed text, traced text, or someone else's work.
- Nothing unlawful, hateful, or harassing, anywhere: submissions, challenges, bios, suggestions.
- Don't spam or flood the bot, the editor, or the Challenge Wizard, and don't try to work around the site's rate limits.
- Don't probe, break, or bypass the site's security or moderation.
- Don't break the rules of the Discord server you submit to.

If content breaks these rules, I may hide or remove it. If an account keeps breaking them, or breaks them badly enough once, I may ban it: a banned account can still browse, but can no longer submit, create or edit challenges, like, or otherwise post to or interact with the site. Creating or using another account to get around a ban isn't allowed, and such accounts will be banned too. This is a small community site, so moderation calls are made with common sense, at my discretion.

## The Challenge Wizard

The optional Challenge Wizard formats challenge text automatically. As described in the [privacy policy](/privacy), the text you type is sent to a third-party service as part of the formatting process. The output can contain mistakes, so check the result before publishing.

Publishing a challenge means you're vouching for it as if you had written it by hand: if it quotes something you had no right to share, or otherwise breaks the rules above, "the wizard produced it" isn't a defense, and the same consequences apply, up to and including removal of the challenge and a ban.
